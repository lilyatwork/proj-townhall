03 Research & Synthesis
🔬

AI as a research partner

AI has the potential to handle volume — surfacing patterns across large datasets of interview notes and transcripts. But the insight still belongs to us.

Research Planning

Drafting user research plans

Used AI to scaffold research plans from a brief — structuring goals, methods, and discussion guides. Saved time on structure. Human judgment was still essential for method selection and question design.

Dovetail, NotebookLLM

Synthesising interview insights

Uploaded transcripts and synthesis notes and asked AI to surface recurring themes. Useful for getting an orientation across a large dataset — not as a replacement for close reading and researcher interpretation.

⚠️ Fact-checking always sits with us. AI-synthesised insights are a starting point — always verify against primary sources before drawing conclusions.
04 Writing with AI
✍️

Writing with AI

From first-draft collaborator to shared vocabulary — here's what we learned about getting useful output, consistently.

AI-Assisted Writing

AI UX writer agent

Building an AI UX writer agent that retrieves pre-approved copy from a library to maintain consistency, and validates copy against approved scheme names and style guidelines. It also handles writing new copy from scratch when needed.

🤝 Treat AI like a new collaborator joining your team: it needs context, constraints, and a clear brief. The more specific you are, the more useful it becomes.
05 Daily Workflow

AI in Your
Daily Workflow

Not every AI use case needs to be a big experiment. Some of the most useful moments happen quietly, day-to-day.

Cowork

Turning plans into visual outlines

Cowork takes a plan or task list and turns it into a structured visual outline — great for kicking off project planning sessions or aligning a team quickly on scope before diving into detailed work. It bridges the gap between a rough idea and a structured brief.
