03 Research & Synthesis
🔬

AI as a research partner

AI has the potential to handle volume — surfacing patterns across large datasets of interview notes and transcripts. But the insight still belongs to us.

Research Planning

Drafting user research plans

Used AI to scaffold research plans from a brief — structuring goals, methods, and discussion guides. Saved time on structure. Human judgment was still essential for method selection and question design.

Dovetail, NotebookLLM

Synthesising interview insights

Uploaded transcripts and synthesis notes and asked AI to surface recurring themes. Useful for getting an orientation across a large dataset — not as a replacement for close reading and researcher interpretation.

⚠️ Fact-checking always sits with us. AI-synthesised insights are a starting point — always verify against primary sources before drawing conclusions.
04 Communication & Content
✍️

AI for Communication
& Content

Writing is where AI has the most immediate day-to-day value — if you treat it like a first-draft collaborator, not a finished writer.

AI-Assisted Writing

Drafts, copy, and communication

We used AI to draft feature announcements, explore UX microcopy variations, and write first cuts of stakeholder update emails. The output is consistently fast and structurally sound. The voice, nuance, and judgment — that's still an edit pass away. The best results came when we gave it specific context, tone guidelines, and clear constraints.

AI writes fast. Good writing still takes time.
05 Lessons Learned
💡

Lessons in
Working with AI

The quality of AI output is directly proportional to the quality of your input. Here's the biggest pattern we noticed across every experiment.

🏷️ Precise naming and shared language matter

Vague prompts produce vague outputs. When our team aligned on shared language — consistent component names, user types, workflow stages — AI outputs became far more relevant, accurate, and reusable across sessions. The effort we put into naming things clearly for ourselves translated directly into better AI collaboration.

🤝 Treat AI like a new collaborator joining your team: it needs context, constraints, and a clear brief. The more specific you are, the more useful it becomes.
Garbage in, garbage out — but more so with AI than with anything else.
06 Daily Workflow
⚙️

AI in Your
Daily Workflow

Not every AI use case needs to be a big experiment. Some of the most useful moments happen quietly, day-to-day.

Cowork

Turning plans into visual outlines

Cowork takes a plan or task list and turns it into a structured visual outline — great for kicking off project planning sessions or aligning a team quickly on scope before diving into detailed work. It bridges the gap between a rough idea and a structured brief.

Try this week Paste your next project brief into an AI tool and ask it to identify the top 3 open questions.
And this Next time you have research notes, ask NotebookLM to surface the top 5 themes — then check them against your own read.
And this Write a one-sentence prompt for a task you do repeatedly. Refine it until the output is actually useful.

AI tools work best when they reduce friction, not add it. Start with a real problem, try a tool, and reflect on what actually changed.

The best AI workflow is the one you'll actually use. 🧡
