🔁 Ideas for the New Product–Design Sync Format

Goal: make the sync feel genuinely useful — a space where everyone feels heard, blockers surface early, and the team leaves with clarity. Here's a suggested structure you can adapt.

🙋 Check-in round (~5 min)

  • One word or one sentence: how is everyone showing up today?
  • Keeps it human, surfaces energy levels
  • Rotate who goes first so it doesn't feel predictable

📣 Designer updates (~10–15 min)

  • Each designer shares: what I'm working on, anything notable
  • Keep it punchy — 2 min max per person
  • Encourage screenshots or a quick Figma frame
  • Async option: pre-fill a shared doc before the sync

🚧 Blockers & support (~5–10 min)

  • "Where do you need input or a second pair of eyes?"
  • Opens space to ask for help without it feeling awkward
  • You or a peer can volunteer to follow up after the call

📌 Product alignment (~5 min)

  • Any product updates that affect design priorities?
  • Upcoming kickoffs, deadlines, or context shifts
  • Helps the team feel in-the-loop, not surprised

💡 Optional: rotating slot (~5 min)

  • Inspiration share, a UX find, a quick retro, a tip
  • One person owns it each week — keeps it fresh
  • Great for the intern or augmented designer to participate

✅ Close-out (~2 min)

  • Any actions? Who owns them?
  • One thing each person is taking away
  • Leave on a positive or energising note
💬 Suggested total runtime: 30–40 min. If your current sync runs long and feels unfocused, try timeboxing each section with a visible timer. You could also run a retro after the first pilot to see what the team wants to keep, drop, or add — makes them co-owners of the format.
